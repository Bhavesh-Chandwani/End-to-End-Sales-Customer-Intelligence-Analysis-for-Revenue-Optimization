# 🚀 End-to-End Sales & Customer Intelligence Analysis for Revenue Optimization

## 📌 Project Overview

This project presents a comprehensive analysis of sales, products, and customer behavior using SQL-based exploratory data analysis (EDA). The goal is to uncover actionable insights that help improve revenue, optimize product strategy, and enhance customer retention.

The dataset consists of:

* **Customer Table:** 18,484 records
* **Product Table:** 295 products
* **Fact Table:** Sales transactions including order date, shipping date, quantity, price, and revenue

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Analyze overall business performance
* Identify top-performing and underperforming products
* Understand customer distribution and behavior
* Evaluate geographical and demographic trends
* Perform time-based sales analysis
* Provide data-driven business recommendations

---

## 📊 Key Metrics

* **Total Sales:** 29.35M
* **Total Orders:** 27,659
* **Total Customers:** 18,484
* **Average Price:** 486

---

## 🌍 Customer Insights

### Country-wise Distribution

* United States contributes the highest customers and sales (~40%)
* Other countries like Canada, France, Germany show lower engagement

👉 **Insight:** Business is heavily dependent on a single geography

---

### Gender Distribution

* Male: 9,341
* Female: 9,128

👉 **Insight:** Balanced distribution → no gender-specific bias

---

### Customer Segmentation

* Regular Customers: 14,839
* New Customers: 1,990
* VIP Customers: 1,655

👉 **Insight:** Very low VIP conversion rate

---

## 🛒 Product Analysis

### Product Distribution

* Components: 127
* Bikes: 97
* Others: Minimal

---

### Revenue Contribution

* Bikes: **96.46% of total revenue**
* Accessories & Clothing: <5%

👉 **Insight:** Extreme dependency on one category

---

### Top Performing Products

* All top products belong to **Mountain Bikes category**

---

### Worst Performing Products

* Socks, patches, and small accessories generate minimal revenue

👉 **Insight:** Long tail of low-performing products

---

## 💰 Pricing Insights

* Bikes are high-ticket items (~949 average cost)
* Other categories are low-cost add-ons

👉 **Insight:** Bikes drive revenue, others support cross-selling

---

## 📈 Sales Trend Analysis

### Yearly Trends

* Significant spike in 2013
* Drop in subsequent years

👉 **Insight:** Inconsistent growth pattern

---

### Monthly Trends

* Fluctuating sales across months

👉 **Insight:** Possible seasonality in sales

---

### Running Total

* Growth exists but lacks stability

---

## 👥 Customer Revenue Analysis

* Top customers generate ~13K revenue each
* Revenue distribution is evenly spread

👉 **Insight:** No strong high-value customer base

---

## 📦 Product Segmentation

* Majority of products fall under mid-range pricing (<500)

👉 **Insight:** Lack of premium and budget diversification

---

## ⚠️ Key Business Problems Identified

* Overdependence on Bikes (96% revenue)
* Low customer retention (many one-time buyers)
* Weak performance of accessories and clothing
* Limited global presence (US-focused)
* Inconsistent sales trends
* Low VIP customer conversion

---

## 🚀 Strategic Business Recommendations

### 1. Increase Customer Value

* Introduce bundling (Bike + Accessories)
* Upselling strategies
* Loyalty programs

---

### 2. Improve Customer Retention

* Email marketing campaigns
* Discounts for repeat purchases
* Personalized recommendations

---

### 3. Diversify Revenue Streams

* Strengthen Accessories & Clothing categories
* Increase contribution to 20–30%

---

### 4. Expand Market Reach

* Target Europe and other regions
* Localized marketing strategies

---

### 5. Optimize Product Portfolio

* Focus on top-performing products
* Remove or bundle low-performing items

---

### 6. Build Premium Strategy

* Introduce high-value product tiers
* Maintain budget-friendly options

---

### 7. Stabilize Sales Growth

* Identify seasonality
* Run targeted campaigns during peak periods

---

## 🧾 Final Summary of Decisions

* Focus on **increasing revenue per customer**
* Reduce risk by **diversifying product categories**
* Improve **customer retention and loyalty**
* Expand beyond the **US market**
* Optimize product mix using **Pareto principle (80/20 rule)**
* Strengthen **high-performing product lines**
* Build **consistent and predictable revenue streams**
